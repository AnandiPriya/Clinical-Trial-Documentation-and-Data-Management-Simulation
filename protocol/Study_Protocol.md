# Study Protocol

## Protocol Title

Clinical Trial Documentation and Data Management Simulation for HPV Screening Study

## Protocol Number

HPV-001

## Version

Version 1.0

## Principal Investigator

Anandi Priya (Simulated Study)

## Background

Human Papillomavirus (HPV) infection is one of the major risk factors associated with cervical cancer. Early detection through molecular screening methods can improve clinical outcomes and support timely intervention.

## Study Objective

To evaluate HPV screening outcomes and demonstrate clinical research documentation and data management procedures in a simulated clinical study environment.

## Study Design

* Study Type: Observational
* Study Setting: Simulated Clinical Research Environment
* Study Duration: 6 Months
* Sample Size: 100 Participants

## Inclusion Criteria

* Female participants aged 21–65 years
* Eligible for HPV screening
* Able to provide informed consent

## Exclusion Criteria

* Incomplete clinical records
* Previous cervical cancer diagnosis
* Withdrawal of consent

## Study Procedures

1. Participant enrollment
2. Informed consent collection
3. Clinical data collection
4. HPV screening assessment
5. Data validation
6. Statistical analysis
7. Final study reporting

## Data Management

* Data will be recorded using Case Report Forms (CRFs).
* Participant confidentiality will be maintained.
* Data validation checks will be performed regularly.
* Access will be restricted to authorized personnel.

## Safety Considerations

* Participant privacy will be protected.
* Any adverse events will be documented and reviewed.

## Regulatory Compliance

This simulated study follows the principles of:

* Good Clinical Practice (GCP)
* ICH-GCP Guidelines
* Data Integrity Standards
* Clinical Documentation Best Practices

## Expected Outcome

This project demonstrates clinical trial documentation, data management workflows, regulatory compliance awareness, and healthcare data analysis skills relevant to clinical research roles.
